# Image Generation Prompt: Bamboo Culm Tip (Topper)

**Target output:** 512 × 1024 PNG (1:2 vertical aspect), solid white background, solid black silhouette.

**Prompt:**

The tapering crown of a bamboo stalk — the natural finishing top of a bamboo cane — rendered as a solid black silhouette on a pure white background, in a hand-drawn ink-wash style consistent with traditional East Asian sumi-e brushwork. This sprite is meant to slot directly on top of a tileable bamboo cane segment to give it a natural ending instead of a sawed cut.

**Trunk composition:**

- A vertical bamboo cane runs from the BOTTOM edge of the image upward, occupying roughly the lower 60% of the image height.
- The cane is centered horizontally with a slight, gentle organic curve or lean (no more than 10% horizontal deviation).
- The cane width matches the existing tileable cane segment style: thick, with 1–2 visible internode joints (dark horizontal banded rings).
- As the cane rises, it tapers gradually — at the top of the trunk section it has narrowed to roughly 50–60% of its base width.
- Above the trunk, the cane continues as a thin, whippy tip — narrowing to a near-point — bending or arcing slightly off-vertical for an organic, growing feel.

**CRITICAL BOTTOM-EDGE REQUIREMENT:**

The cane must extend cleanly off the BOTTOM edge of the canvas — do NOT taper, do NOT show a cut, do NOT add a node ring at the very bottom edge. The bottom edge of the cane should match the top of the existing tileable cane segment: same horizontal position (centered), same width, same ink density. When this culm-tip sprite is placed directly on top of a cane segment, the two should appear as one continuous bamboo with no visible seam.

**Foliage:**

- 2–4 small leaf branches sprout from the upper trunk and the thin whippy tip.
- Branches alternate sides — NOT clustered or stacked.
- Leaves are sharp, tapered, lanciform — matching the existing single-leaf and leaf-cluster sprites' style.
- Leaves are sparse enough that the curving tip of the cane remains visible above the topmost leaves (the cane reaches above the foliage to a clear point).
- A few leaves may droop slightly downward; most fan outward and upward from their branch nodes.

**Style:**

- Solid black silhouette with subtle ink-bleed irregularity along edges (organic, hand-painted, not vector-perfect).
- Faint vertical brush-stroke texture inside the cane (slightly lighter charcoal grey) to suggest cane fiber. Internode rings rendered fully solid black for contrast.
- The composition should feel like one continuous brushstroke from base to tip — a natural growth gesture.

**Background:** Pure white (#FFFFFF), uniform, no gradients, no noise, no shadows.

**Negative:** No additional stalks. No ground or base detail. No animals. No text. No frame. No watermark. No color other than black and white. No bunched leaf clusters at the very top — the cane tip should pierce above the leaves into clear sky.
