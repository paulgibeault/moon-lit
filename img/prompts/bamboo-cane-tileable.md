# Image Generation Prompt: Tileable Bamboo Cane Segment

**Target output:** 512 × 1536 PNG (1:3 vertical aspect), solid white background, solid black silhouette.

**Prompt:**

A single section of bamboo cane silhouette, rendered as a solid black shape on a pure white background, in a hand-drawn ink-wash style consistent with traditional East Asian sumi-e brushwork. The cane runs vertically through the entire image from top edge to bottom edge with NO leaves, NO branches, and NO foliage of any kind — just the bare trunk.

The trunk is centered horizontally with a slight, gentle organic curve (no more than 8% horizontal deviation from the vertical centerline). It contains exactly THREE visible internode joints (the dark horizontal banded rings characteristic of bamboo), evenly spaced along the length: one near the top quarter, one in the middle, and one near the bottom quarter. The trunk width is consistent top to bottom (no significant taper), approximately 14–18% of the image width.

**CRITICAL TILING REQUIREMENT:** The top edge and bottom edge of the cane must be IDENTICAL in horizontal position, width, and ink density, so that two copies of this image stacked vertically read as one continuous cane with no visible seam. The cane must extend cleanly off the top edge and bottom edge of the canvas — do NOT taper to a point, do NOT show a cut or termination, do NOT add a node ring at the very top or very bottom edge (place node rings only in the interior so seams stay clean).

The silhouette is solid black with subtle ink-bleed irregularity along the edges (slightly rough, organic, hand-painted feel — not vector-perfect). The interior may show faint vertical brush-stroke texture in slightly lighter charcoal grey to suggest cane fiber, but contrast remains high.

**Background:** Pure white (#FFFFFF), uniform, no gradients, no noise, no shadows.

**Negative:** No leaves. No branches. No bamboo shoots. No animals. No text. No frame. No watermark. No color other than black and white.
