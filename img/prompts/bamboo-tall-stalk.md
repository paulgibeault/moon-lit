# Image Generation Prompt: Tall Full-Height Bamboo Stalk

**Target output:** 512 × 3072 PNG (1:6 vertical aspect), solid white background, solid black silhouette.

**Prompt:**

A single tall, slender bamboo stalk rendered as a solid black silhouette on a pure white background, spanning the full height of a very vertical canvas, in a hand-drawn ink-wash style consistent with traditional East Asian sumi-e brushwork. This is a complete self-contained stalk meant to be placed in the foreground of a scene as a framing element.

**Trunk composition:**
- The trunk runs vertically the entire length of the image, with a subtle natural curve (an organic S-shape or gentle lean, not perfectly straight — horizontal deviation up to 12% of image width).
- The trunk tapers gradually narrower toward the top (top width is roughly 60–70% of base width).
- Contains 6–8 visible internode rings (the dark horizontal banded joints of bamboo), with natural spacing variation — slightly closer together near the top, slightly further apart in the middle.
- The base extends cleanly off the bottom edge (no termination — the stalk should appear to continue below the canvas).
- The top tapers to a soft point or a final small leaf flourish near the upper edge.

**Leaf branches:**
- 3–4 small leaf clusters branch off the main trunk at varied heights, NOT evenly spaced (avoid any visible repeating pattern).
- Suggested placement: one branch in the upper third, one near the middle, one in the lower third, and optionally one more for asymmetric variation.
- Each cluster is small relative to the trunk (cluster width roughly 30–50% of image width), with 2–4 lanciform leaves fanning outward from a short side stem.
- Leaf branches alternate sides — do not stack all branches on the same side.
- Leaves are sharp, tapered, ink-wash style consistent with the existing single-leaf and leaf-cluster sprites.

**Style:**
- Solid black silhouette with subtle ink-bleed irregularity along edges (organic, hand-painted, not vector-perfect).
- Faint vertical brush-stroke texture inside the trunk in slightly lighter charcoal grey to suggest cane fiber, with the internode rings rendered in fully solid black for contrast.
- The overall stalk should feel like one continuous brushstroke composition — naturally grown, not assembled.

**Background:** Pure white (#FFFFFF), uniform, no gradients, no noise, no shadows.

**Negative:** No additional stalks. No ground or base detail. No animals. No text. No frame. No watermark. No color other than black and white. No multiple stalks side by side — exactly ONE stalk through the center of the canvas.
