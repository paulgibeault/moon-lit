# Image Generation Prompt: Bamboo Root / Base Section

**Target output:** 768 × 768 PNG (1:1 square), solid white background, solid black silhouette.

**Prompt:**

The base of a bamboo stalk where it meets the ground, rendered as a solid black silhouette on a pure white background, in a hand-drawn ink-wash style consistent with traditional East Asian sumi-e brushwork. This is the foreground anchor element where bamboo emerges from the earth or shoreline.

The bamboo trunk extends from the TOP of the image downward, occupying roughly the upper 70% of the image as a vertical cane with two visible internode rings. The trunk widens slightly as it descends (gentle flare, roughly 1.2× the upper width at its base) and meets a clustered base where it transitions into the ground. At the bottom 30% of the image, show a few short bamboo shoots or culm sheaths peeling away from the main stalk at angles (3–5 small triangular protrusions), and a suggestion of low grass blades or reed fronds fanning out from the base in a horizontal spread roughly 1.5× the trunk width.

**CRITICAL TILING REQUIREMENT:** The TOP edge of the trunk must match the bottom edge of the companion tileable cane segment — same horizontal position (centered), same width (14–18% of image width), same ink density, NO node ring at the very top edge. The cane should continue cleanly off the top of the canvas so it joins seamlessly with a cane segment stacked above.

The BOTTOM of the image fades naturally into the ground plane — the grass and shoot details should form an irregular bottom edge rather than a hard horizontal line, so it composites naturally over any background.

The silhouette is solid black with the same ink-bleed irregularity and faint vertical brush-stroke texture as the cane segment, for visual consistency.

**Background:** Pure white (#FFFFFF), uniform, no gradients, no noise, no shadows.

**Negative:** No leaves on the trunk. No leaf clusters. No upper foliage. No animals. No text. No frame. No watermark. No color other than black and white. No water, rocks, or other scenery — just the bamboo base and its immediate grass.
